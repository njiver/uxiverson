Monumental Art

Publication | 240 Pages | Reflections on the Stupas at Sanchi and the Buddhist Caves at Ajanta

Overview

We walked to Bodhihana’s land, through a gnarled gate, down a tree lined road, onto a worn path in the grass. A man was pacing around a huge tree. One light underneath. It was hard to see at dusk. We were greeted by the grin of the white-bearded man. “Welcome to my office, this is my boss, this tree, my boss, my friend.”

My true pleasure is in meeting the people. It is magnetic. It can be something for everyone if they choose to see it that way. It gives you more energy. The pleasures of life… It has taught me to use my gift first by learning from silence. One gift. Not arts or writing but speaking and opening my heart.

- Bodhihana from Sanchi
