Garden Pro

CONCEPT | Instant answers for your gardening questions

Overview

Garden Pro is a one-stop app for gardening information and expert advice from local Master Gardeners.

The American Horticultural Society desires to generate awareness of master gardeners hoping to share their knowledge and make a positive impact in their communities.

Problem

Before Garden Pro, persona Sara Miller was overwhelmed trying to find the right plant info for her. With it, Sara gets plant care tips directly from Master Gardeners and can do research for her garden without worry.

My Role

Create a minimum viable product to meet the needs of the AHS. Plan and execute a design through research, planning, design, and iterations with team of three designers. I took on a role as a UX researcher and designer.


Research and Planning

UX Process

We agreed upon a functional team plan and decision making process. Then collaborated for user research, planning, design, and testing.

User Interview and Affinity Mapping

Together we screened and interviewed six users then distilled a set of data points to create a persona representing a beginner gardener. I organized this into a project summary and created a task analysis. From this we generated a persona hypothesis Sara.

Persona

The persona Sara Miller came to fruition through user interview data points to lead the focus of the design.

I have an intimate connection with gardening. I am happy when my plant is happy and sad when it isn't doing well.

Design Studio, Scenarios, User Flows, and Sitemap

The team and I began time-boxed design studios to create a solution for Sara. We discussed and consolidated ideas for a minimum viable product that would meet her needs. With the consolidated sketches we then created storyboards, scenarios, user flows, and a sitemap to align user and organizational goals for with the mobile experience.


Design and Testing

Wireframes

We began a first round of testing and iteration with paper prototypes. We reconvened With the knowledge we collected to distilled the data points and iterated the design with wireframes.

Usability Testing and Iteration

We ran a second round of testing with an interactive Axure prototype. We again reconvened and discussed the next steps for iteration.

Project Handoff

At the end of three weeks of research, planning, design, and testing we had produced an Axure wireframe prototype then presented our process and final deliverables.


Insights and Next Steps

Design for the User

I discovered that users were able to easily complete conventional tasks like doing a search query but had diffculty navigating tabbed menus. We revised the menu's to alleviate ambiguities.

Our team focused on a clear goal from beginning to end: design for the user. We set a clear work pattern always communicating a clear goal for the next steps we anticipated to take as a group and as individual contributors.
