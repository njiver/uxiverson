The Curated Travel

Client Project | Improving an existing experience

Overview

The Curated Travel is a boutique travel site dedicated to providing expertly customized travel experiences for its guests across Europe, Asia, and America.

I approached them to create new content for India, Vietnam, and the American West, in addition to providing recommendations on how to tighten up the user experience of their site.

Tasks

• Provide expert travel content and photography for India, Vietnam, and the American West.
• Provide recommendations from a UX perspective in order to tighten up the experience.
• Contribute feedback and input on future design features.

My Role

My role with the Curated Travel is as a remote content writer, photographer, and UX contributor.


Inspiring travel to India, Vietnam, and the American West

Considerations: What do guests want to experience?

I aligned content with the goals of the brand to inspire travel.

My purpose in creating content is to provide expert level experiences for guests. This revolves around three major things: amazing experiences, culture, and food.

I started by writing 'day by day' travelogues based on firsthand experiences and assembling mood boards from my personal travel photo collection to bring the places to life.

Domain Research

I asked many questions to guide the content. What do the guests search for in an amazing experience, specifically in India, Vietnam, or the American West? Are they looking for landscapes, cities, architecture, a journey, a weekend, unexpected surprises, comfort, adventure, personal accounts? How are they searching? Why does a piece of content inspire them? How do I tell a story that excites their interest? How does this fit into the vision that the site has for itself?

Think with Google has assembled amazing research about how Travel Micro-Moments fit into the customer journey:

85% of leisure travelers decide on activities only after having arrived at the destination

According to Think with Google there are 4 main micro-moments, I-want-to-get-away, Time-to-make-a-plan, Let's-book-it, and Can't-wait-to-explore.

An important goal is to capture these moments as travelers engage with content. Travelers are interested in photos and points of interest but first they need the content to show up in their searches. In these moments content must be relevent to the traveler.

Over 40% of travelers say they bounce back and forth between dreaming about and planning their next trip—zooming in on the details for one destination and then zooming out to reconsider all the options again… 40% of US travel site visits come from mobile.

Tightening up the UX and UI

Do guests achieve satisfaction of their goals and needs?

Travelers are more apt to book with sites that satisfy their goals and needs. A trend also shown in research from Think with Google explains that travelers are more loyal to needs than brands. The majority of travelers, 72%, just want the most relevant information, and 88% of travelers would switch to another site if the first doesn't satisfy their needs.

Understanding this, it should take minimal effort for guests to find what they need in order to be inspired to travel and to book with the site. I conducted a heuristic analysis to examine the user flow from clicking into the site from an ad, and also planning a trip. I discovered cases of issues and opportunities related to style, formatting, and usability.


Issues and Opportunities

After completing an analysis of the site I documented the specific cases where improvements could be made, and also provided some general recommendations:

Maintain the Elegance – Establish an attractive font hierarchy. Paragraph text needs to be legible, 12pt to 16pt, and for informational text sans-serif is preferred.

Handpick the Best Photos – Photos which are featured on the site should 'scream' travel. Top destinations should be matched with inspiring photos.

Focus the Layout – The content and layout needs to follow a recognizable pattern. Keep it consistent and pleasing throughout. Include calls to action which guide the needs and goals of the guests.

Minimalist Approach – Include what is needed and remove distractions. One compelling element with a clear purpose is more effective than many elements without a clear purpose.

Tell a Story – Consider each element as an intentional, compelling moment that will motivate or inspire. Help the guests achieve their goals in a way that is fluid, easy, and delightful.




For example, a specific case which had great opportunities for improvement was the Plan Your Trip section. The first iteration was a ten step survey overlayed on an image, which automatically recommended an itinerary package in order for the curating service to begin. This was greatly improved by reducing the path to 3 steps from the landing page, by using photos which were relevant to the itinerary packages, by returning control to the guest, and by focusing the questions which were asked.


Offering Pre-Curated options

Mockups for Destination Guide Content

Guests were asking for pre-curated options in addition to custom itineraries. I produced wireframes, mockups, and redlines for the new destination guide feature that aligned with the site's photo-centric, minimalist style. Scrolling landing pages enhance discoverability of content and present guests with more information on when to go, where to go, pre-curated itineraries to book, and important travel advice for each region.
