Capital One Managed Portfolio

Client Project | Bringing a human touch to the mobile experience

Overview

Capital One Managed Portfolio is an IOS app for millennials that helps users check-in on their investment via their mobile device.

My team and I designed a simple experience that not only helps customers check-in on their money but also helps them see how they are on track for their goals, understand their investment, and stay in touch with their financial advisors.

Problem

Capital One asked us design the mobile experience for its new Managed Portfolios:
• Give millennials and a window into what their money is doing and how it is doing.
• Help millennials achieve their goals and stay in touch with their investment.

My Role

I took on a role as a UX researcher, designer, and project documentation lead. The project team and I met with Capital One to plan the scope and objectives for the experience. We scheduled a design sprint for research, planning, design, and testing for an IOS prototype using InVision.


Research and Planning

UX Process

Domain Research and Competitive/Comparative Analysis

My team and I created a competitive / comparative analysis on several products in the domain. We researched Capital One's suite of mobile products and reviewed user tests for the desktop experience to gain a better understanding of how to translate the experience to mobile.

Creating Personas from User Interview Data

I created an interview screener, survey, and interview script. I conducted user interviews with my team which included external participants, current customers, and stakeholders. We distilled the interview data through affinity mapping.

Persona Hypotheses

Insights from this allowed the team to create two persona hypotheses for the mobile experience. Our research aligned with a demographic of millennials who trust in technology and who are beginner investors.

I want to see how my money is projected to grow over time, but after I make the initial investment I want it to be low maintenance.

Aligning the User's Goals with Capital One's Objectives

We created scenarios to align user research with Capital One's design objectives and then planned out where the mobile experience lives in the user’s journey with the product.


Design and Testing

Design Studio

We considered patterns already set by Capital One’s banking app and included conventions from their style guides to create a first iteration focused on the personas.

Paper Prototypes and User Testing

In our first round of user testing we used paper prototypes. In each test we documented interactions with video recording of our participants. With the initial tests complete, we met for a whiteboarding session to develop the second iteration.

Client Meeting Feedback

We met with stakeholders at Capital One to gain feedback on the initial user testing results and our first and second iterations. Then discussed how to implement the improvements which the team at Capital One considered to be most important.

Low Fidelity Wireframes and Continued User Testing

We outlined the next iteration of improvements then constructed wireframes from user testing data and the recommendations from stakeholders. New design decisions included advisor scheduling, chat, and deposit withdrawal flows which were consistent with Capital One's investment advising objectives.

High Fidelity Wireframes and Remote Testing

In the next round of testing and iteration we brought the prototype into high fidelity and tested remotely on usertesting.com. Participants rated the prototype an average of 4.6 out of 5 for this question: "How satisfied would you be using this application for your automated investments?"


Key refinements in the fifth and sixth iterations
• Clean visual signifiers for the user's investment allocation
• Instant feedback for the user on how account actions may affect their investment
• Automated answerbot to handle FAQ call in volume for the advising team


Project Handoff

We delivered a high fidelity interactive prototype along with our project documentation from three weeks of research, planning, design, and testing to the stakeholders at Capital One. We then presented our process and final deliverable to the client.


Insights and Next Steps

Communication, Involvement, and Agreement

Provide clear and consistent communication, involvement, and agreement between stakeholders and the design team. Involve the client in the design process when new steps are being made. Don’t let questions go unanswered. Know what you want to ask and why. Ask the client if they have a style guide and if they can provide it for the team. Use style guides and typeface hierarchy to guide visual design and create a stronger, consistent style. Consider accessibility in design decisions.
