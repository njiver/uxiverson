Icons for an Online Marketplace

Client Project | Creating a new style for the icons



Overview

New icons and glyphs for an online marketplace.

Objectives

• Create 41+ icons for the online marketplace including material icons and glyphs.
• Icons must be recognizable between 64pt size and 12pt size.
• Icons will be clear signifiers for the site navigation and actions.
• Style will follow current design trends, have unity, and artistic themes.

My Role

As the Visual Designer my role is to create attractive new icons inspired by current design trends. The purpose for the icons is to provide useful visual information to users of the site and to boost usability. My tools used are pencil, paper, Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop.


Research and Planning

Design Process

Define. Research. Create. Refine. Deliver. In each stage I ask for feedback from the stakeholder and plan a schedule for my work. One of my goals is to create a useful and attractive family of icons.

Trending Styles

Two current visual trends in web design are material and flat design. Monochromic glyph icons often also take on an embossed or flat style, a stroke or fill style.



Resources and Familiar Conventions

I used iconfinder, the noun project, dribble, and image search to search trending conventions. Google's material design guide was also a valuable resource. For an action like sharing I find it useful to consider icons that are already familiar to users.





Design and Iteration



Developing the Metaphor

Each icon communicates its purpose through its depiction of an action, feature, or metaphor. I begin by doing a mind-mapping exercise to quickly explore ideas. I'll then narrow the ideas, sketch again, iterate, and gain feedback.



Grid and Keylines

In the next stage I'll sketch the designs on a 16x16 pixel grid for scalability. Keylines on the grid constrain the proportion and geometric consistency of the icons.



Designing the Vector

With the feedback I will narrow the sketches further then transfer them into Illustrator to create a vector graphic. I'll iterate on the vectors, create a mockup on the site UI, and gain more feedback.



Refinement and Handoff

After I've pinned down the style I'll create simple spec in Illustrator to follow with future designs. I'll make sure that the vectors are legible from the smallest size to the largest. I'll narrow the iterations, refine the designs, and gain feedback again. If they're ready then I'll hand them off as finished icons.


Insights and Next Steps

Design with Intent, Establish a Style, Gather Feedback

Having clear design intent and objectives guide the design process. Understand why you are designing, define how you will design, then create a vision for it. Give yourself space to explore and iterate but establish a style to unify the icons. Integrate feedback into the design schedule so that the stakeholder is involved throughout the design process.
