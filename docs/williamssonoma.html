Williams-Sonoma Cooking School

CONCEPT | Bringing good taste home

Overview

Williams-Sonoma Cooking School is designed for users interested in new cooking skills, recipes, and kitchenware.

Cooking classes that are currently available for customers need to be integrated with the online shopping experience and provide to them a way to track thier purchases with an interactive checklist.

Problem

Before this site, persona Chris Taylor struggled to find a unified experience with Williams-Sonoma for booking his courses, finding his recipes, talking to pro chefs, and purchasing relevant products. With it Chris can now easily do this with confidence.

My Role

Design for Williams-Sonoma and its customers to create a minimum viable product that meets thier needs. Plan and execute a design through research, planning, design, and iterations. I took on a role as a UX researcher and designer.


Research and Planning

UX Process

I created a schedule of design sprint objectives and set to work conducting user research, planning, design, and testing.

User Interview, Affinity Mapping, and Persona

I screened, organized, and conducted user interviews to synthesize a persona from interview data. I used affinity mapping to identify key points that would fulfill the needs of the user.

There's a different feeling to doing things fresh and correctly, when you know what you are doing is tasting good you just think this is great!

Comparative / Competitive Analysis

I identified domain opportunities for Williams-Sonoma Cooking School through organizational research and a comparative / competitive analysis. I discovered that information for cooking courses at Williams-Sonoma lives on two separate sites. It needed to be unified so that all courses could be easily searched and accessed from one place.

Using UX design methods to improve the experience

Planning the site required user concept maps, card sorting, storyboarding, and user flows. Each brought me a step closer to improving the Williams-Sonoma experience for Chris.


Design and Testing

Paper Prototypes

I focused in on the needs of the persona by testing and iterating with paper prototypes. Paper prototyping allowed me to rapidly test and validate the elements of the design.

Usability Testing and Iteration

Following the paper testing I developed grayscale wireframes and a wireframed prototype in InVision. I then created a test script and scheduled a second round of testing for the wireframed prototype.


Insights and Next Steps

Anticipate expectations through research and testing

Testing revealed that users delighted in the task flow where they felt in control of the interaction and were to achieve their goal without being blocked.

In usability tests, pay close attention to the intention of the script, and the tasks that you want the participants to complete. It can reveal valuable information about how to improve the user flow in the next iteration.
