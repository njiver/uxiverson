Kelp

CONCEPT | An app for organizing your favorite places

Overview

Kelp is a bookmark organizing app for people who need a more personalized way of tracking their places.

It was designed for my friend Mariah so that she could easily catalogue her favorite places, her new places, and places she has already been to.

Problem

Before Kelp, Mariah had difficulty making a quick decision for where to go because bookmarking features in her other favorite apps did not meet her needs. With Kelp she can quickly decide from her self-curated catalogue.

My Role

To understand Mariah's real needs through user interview, then plan and design a unique app to help her in her daily life. I took on a role as a UX resercher and designer.


Research and Planning

UX Process

When I first met Mariah my role was to understand her habits, goals, needs, pains, and routine. I interviewed her to find out more.

User Interview, Affinity Mapping, and Persona

I used the information from her interview in an affinity map, which lead me to insights and allowed me to create a persona hypotheses. I included insights from interviewing and affinity mapping to propose a solution for her problem.

When I am going out with friends I'm usually the one who decides where to go.

Comparative / Competitive Analysis

I then conducted a comparative analysis of her favorite place finding apps. I found it surprising that no major players in the domain offered the bookmarking organization that she really needed.

Design and Testing

User Flow and Clickable Prototype

I sketched out user flow and screenflow for main functions of the app. After iterating the sketches I created clickable prototype in Invision.


Insights and Next Steps

Distill interview data in order to plan a design solution

Distill insights from user interviews and research. Then with user flows, scenarios, and storyboards plan how to organize these into an actionable design solution.
